Why does V stay V? With these tiny primes, 22 happens to be a "fixed point": 225 and 2229 both come back to 22 mod 35. Real RSA uses primes hundreds of digits long, so fixed points and look-alikes essentially never matter.

5. Why it's secret

The eavesdropper sees the public key 35 and 5, and the scrambled JVJ. To read it, they need d=29 — and the only way to get d is to know φ=24, which means knowing the secret primes 5 and 7 that multiply to 35.

Factoring 35 back into 5×7 is trivial here. But for a 600-digit n, factoring it would take classical computers longer than the age of the universe. That factoring step is exactly what Shor's algorithm on a quantum computer would shortcut — the thread we were pulling on.
